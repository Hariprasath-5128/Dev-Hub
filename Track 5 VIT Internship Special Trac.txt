Digital Twin of human Health (virtual health model):
	Imagine your system as a **smart visual health scanner** rather than just an app. When the user clicks **“Analyze”**, the screen subtly transitions into a focused diagnostic mode: the entire 2D human body fades into a dim, almost shadow-like silhouette, while the system “scans” the body with a smooth animation (like a wave moving from head to toe). Within a second, the scan locks onto the problem area—say the chest—and that region begins to **glow with a pulsing red light**, immediately drawing attention.

	A **soft circular ring forms around the affected area**, expanding slightly and then stabilizing, as if marking a target. From this circle, a **clean animated line extends outward** to a floating label box that appears beside the body. This label doesn’t just show text—it presents a **mini explanation panel**:

* “High cardiovascular risk detected”
* “Irregular heart rate pattern”
* “SpO₂ fluctuation observed”

	At the same time, the highlighted region can use a **heatmap-style gradient** (yellow → orange → red) to indicate severity, making the visualization feel more medical and data-driven. To enhance realism, the glowing region can have a **pulse animation synchronized like a heartbeat**, reinforcing the idea of a living system being monitored.

	If multiple issues are detected, the system intelligently highlights **multiple regions with different colors**, each with its own animated circle and labeled explanation, creating a layered diagnostic view. Users can click on any highlighted part to **expand deeper insights**, such as trends (“risk increased over last 3 hours”) or simple recommendations.

	To elevate the experience further, you can add a **voice summary feature** where the system calmly explains:

	> “Attention: Elevated heart stress detected. Immediate monitoring recommended.”

	The background UI remains minimal and dark-themed, ensuring full focus on the body visualization, giving it a **professional medical dashboard feel**. The overall interaction should feel smooth, responsive, and slightly cinematic—so when you demo it, it feels less like a student project and more like a **real AI-powered healthcare product**.
